Yakitori Skewers That Make You Cry

What is Yakitori Skewers

They're Japan's pride and joy when it comes to their mastery over the charcoal grill! The word "yakitori" means "grilled chicken".

The chicken pieces used for these skewers are generally dark meat that contain various types of the chicken such as thigh, breast, liver, or skin. It's so good!

Ingredients

  • 10 wooden skewers
  • 4 skinless,boneless chicken thighs,cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 scallions , sliced into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup sake
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoon mirin
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil,or to taste

Steps

  1. Soak 10 wooden skewers in cold water for 15 minutes.
  2. Thread chicken pieces onto the soaked skewers, alternating with scallions.
  3. Combine sake, soy suace, mirin, and sugar in a small saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes. Reserve 1/2 of the sauce for dipping.
  4. Heat a grill pan over high heat and lightly brush with vegetable oil. Add skewers and cook until chicken is longer pink in the center, basting frequently with 1/2 of the sauce, 7 to 10 minutes per side.
